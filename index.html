Tehzeen

/teh-ZEEN/
Pronunciation: teh (as in "ten") - zeen (rhymes with "mean")
Etymology: A humorous portmanteau of the Urdu word "teh" (تہ), meaning fold or layer, and the English word "jeans."

Meaning:
Noun. A playfully ironic term for a pair of jeans that are excessively folded or cuffed at the bottom, often to a comical or impractical degree. This can be due to the jeans being too long, a deliberate fashion choice, or simply laziness in hemming.

Adjective. Describing someone who is wearing excessively folded jeans.

Verb. (Informal) To excessively fold or cuff one's jeans.

Synonyms: (Informal) Folded to the max, cuffed crazy, layered legs
Antonyms: Hemmed, tailored, appropriately sized
Usage: Primarily used in casual conversation among Urdu speakers and those familiar with Urdu slang. Often carries a lighthearted or slightly mocking tone.
See also: Cuffing, stacking, high-waters

"Look at Ahmed with his tehzeen! He must have folded those jeans up to his knees."

"Don't be so tehzeen, just get them hemmed properly."

"He spent ten minutes tehzeening his new pair of denim."
